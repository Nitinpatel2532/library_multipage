Greenwich Library

Mission of greenwich Library

The mission of Greenwich Library is to provide exceptional resources, programs and services that promote the joy of lifelong learning and discovery, and to offer a welcoming place for people to gather and share experiences.

A few words About Us

The Greenwich Library system consists of the Main Library and Byram Shubert and Cos Cob branches. With 1,800 programs and events per year, the Library serves as the cultural and intellectual crossroads of the community.

This Plan was developed with significant collaboration between Library Staff, Board of Trustees, Friends of Greenwich Library, Friends of the Byram Shubert Library, and Friends of Cos Cob Library.

Library History

Learn more about the storied past of the Greenwich Library. Throughout the 1800s, under the names Greenwich Town Library and Greenwich Reading Room and Library Association, a book-lending institution existed in such locations as the Second Congregational Church and on the second floor of what is now 113 Greenwich Ave.
